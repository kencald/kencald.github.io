User Experience Research & Design

University of Michigan + social content strategy

Summer 2018

In my role at U-M Information and Technology Services, I initiated a content strategy project to reinvigorate ITS’s use of Twitter as supported by quantitative research. As social media lead for my unit, I created a project charge, sought leadership approval, then led a team of interns through a crash-course in UX research methodology. The structure I imposed included periods of scoping and discovery, audience analysis, and content design. Ultimately, we produced a set of detailed guidelines and evidence-based recommendations for contributing staff writers to build engagement with the target audience.

UMSI Design Clinic

Winter 2018

I volunteered for UMSI Design Clinic (now called infoLab) to bring my user experience research and design skills to fruition outside of the classroom. What an experience! I learned so much through a rigorous research process: qualitative interviews, card sorting exercises, comparative user tests, and more. I even contributed to the team’s more concrete deliverables like a user journey and a web-based, interactive prototype for our client—a student startup called Canopy.

The Canopy-Design Clinic team from L–R: Ken Caldwell, Brandon Keelan (client contact), Chuhan Hou, Huyen Phan, Mandy Shen, Chen Li, and Aalap Doshi (group mentor).

Contextual Inquiry

In fall 2017, I worked in a consulting capacity as a student of SI 501 to study the internal team dynamics at the Center for Entrepreneurship (CFE), part of the U-M College of Engineering. Through the project, my team and I developed research protocols and used tried-and-true methods like affinity diagramming to identify key findings. As the project culminated, I led an in-person meeting to recommend ways their team could more effectively collaborate and communicate. The client was very grateful, noting that our “objective and professional manner prompted conversation that wouldn't have otherwise occurred.”
