User Experience Research & Design

Here is a sample of my work.

Social media content strategy

This summer 2018, in my role at U-M Information and Technology Services, I initiated a content strategy project to reinvigorate ITS’s use of Twitter as supported by quantitative research. I created a project charge, sought leadership approval, then led a team of interns through a crash-course in UX research methodology. The structure I imposed included periods of scoping and discovery, audience analysis, and content design. Ultimately, we produced a set of detailed guidelines for contributing staff writers to build engagement with the target audience.

UMSI Design Clinic (now infoLab)

In winter 2018, I volunteered for UMSI Design Clinic to bring my user experience research and design skills to fruition outside of the classroom. What an experience! I learned so much through a rigorous research process: qualitative interviews, card sorting exercises, comparative user tests, and more. I even contributed to the team’s more concrete deliverables like a user journey and a web-based, interactive prototype for our client—a student startup called Canopy.

Contextual Inquiry

In fall 2017, I worked in a consulting capacity as a student of SI 501 to study the internal team dynamics at the Center for Entrepreneurship (CFE), part of the U-M College of Engineering. Through the project, my team and I developed research protocols and used tried-and-true methods like affinity diagramming to identify key findings. As the project culminated, I led an in-person meeting to recommend ways their team could more effectively collaborate and communicate. The client was very grateful, noting that our “objective and professional manner prompted conversation that wouldn't have otherwise occurred.”
